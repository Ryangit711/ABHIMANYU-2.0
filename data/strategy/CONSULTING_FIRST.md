## The Positioning (for every consulting application)

### The One-Sentence Pitch
"I built the centralized operations infrastructure for a multi-site organization — scaling from 3 to 70 employees across 32 locations, growing revenue from $0 to $4M ARR, and delivering a $17M exit. I don't need to learn how your business works — I need to make it work better."

### The Core Narrative
"Aman is not an operator who wants to be a consultant. Aman is a consultant who happened to operate. Every system he built — from 3 to 70+ employees, from $0 to $4M ARR, from 1 location to 32 — was a consulting engagement that he also had to live inside. The difference between a consultant and an operator is: the consultant walks away after the deck. Aman stayed and made it real. That is rarer. That is more valuable."

### The M&A Story
"I directed a $17M acquisition integration: 8 due diligence workstreams, Day 1 readiness planning, 90-day systems consolidation, 100% key talent retention through structured change management. This is what I'd bring to your clients."

### The P&L Story
"I managed full P&L for a $4M organization across 12 departments. I developed multi-scenario financial models, departmental budgets, and variance analysis. I speak C-suite fluently."

## The Consulting Entry Path

```
Month 1: Apply to EY, CGI, Deloitte, PwC, Slalom
         ↓
Month 2: Follow up. Network. Practice cases.
         ↓
Month 3: Interview. Callback. Offer.
         ↓
Month 4: Start. First 90 days = drinking from the fire hose.
         ↓
Year 1:  Build. Systematize. Add value.
         ↓
Year 2:  Step back into advisory role within the firm.
```

**The goal:** Foot in the door. Then roam.
