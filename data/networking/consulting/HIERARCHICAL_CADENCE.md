# Consulting Networking Cadence — Hierarchical Seepage Strategy

**Strategy:** You don't ask one person for a referral. You build a web of relationships across 4 tiers at each firm, letting each conversation feed the next. The referral emerges naturally because multiple people at the firm know your name, your story, and your capability.

## The 4-Tier Hierarchy

| Tier | Level | Title Examples | Purpose | Connect Ease | Referral Power |
|:----:|-------|---------------|---------|:------------:|:--------------:|
| **1** | IC / Consultant | Consultant, Sr Consultant, Manager | Intel · Culture · Firm vocabulary | 🟢 High | Low (but critical as warm intro to Tier 2) |
| **2** | Sr Manager / A Partner | Sr Mgr, Associate Partner, Director | Practice intel · Role insights · Org structure | 🟡 Medium | Medium |
| **3** | Partner / MD | Partner, Managing Director, Practice Lead | Strategic connect · Name recognition | 🔴 Low | High |
| **4** | Recruiter / TA | Talent Acquisition, Recruitment Lead | Direct funnel · Role pipeline · Speed | 🟢 High | Application-level only |

## The Seepage Cadence (Per Firm)

**Phase 1 (Week 1-2):** Tier 1 → Connect + learn. Gather firm intel.
**Phase 2 (Week 2-3):** Tier 2 → Connect + reference what Tier 1 taught you.
**Phase 3 (Week 3-4):** Circle back to Tier 1 → Ask for warm intro to Tier 3 or referral.
**Phase 4 (Week 4-5):** Tier 3 → Connect via Tier 1's warm intro. Ask for referral.
**Parallel:** Tier 4 (Recruiter) — submit application + connect simultaneously.

## Why This Works

- **Tier 1** people are NOT flooded with referral requests. When you build genuine rapport, they remember you.
- **Tier 2** people respect that you talked to their juniors first — shows you understand consulting culture.
- **Tier 3** people only respond to warm intros from their own team. Tier 1 supplies that.
- **The seepage:** By the time you ask for a referral, 2-3 people at the firm already know you by name. The partner hears: "Yeah, I've heard of that person — [Tier 1 name] mentioned him."

# PSYCHOLOGICAL PRINCIPLES

1. **The helper identity:** Consultants see themselves as mentors. Asking for advice activates their self-image. Asking for a job activates their gatekeeper mode. Always ask for advice first.

2. **The reciprocity loop:** When someone gives you advice, they become invested in you. Referring you becomes a validation of their own judgment. "I saw this person early, I helped them, and now they're my colleague."

3. **The social proof seepage:** Tier 1 conversations don't stay private. People talk internally. When you connect with 3 people at the same firm, the name starts circulating. By the time you apply, you're not a stranger.

4. **The warm intro multiplier:** A warm intro from a team member to a partner is worth 10x a cold application. The partner's first thought is "if [employee] vouches for this person, they're probably good."

5. **The "not asking" paradox:** The people who get referrals are the ones who DON'T lead with "can you refer me." They build the relationship, establish credibility, and the referral flows naturally.
