# Networking Playbook — The Introvert's Guide

**Philosophy:** LinkedIn is async. No small talk. No rooms. No handshakes. You write when fresh. You respond when ready. You control the pace. The research advantage: before connecting with anyone, you can spend 5 minutes reading their profile, their posts, their career path. By the time you DM them, you already know what to say.

---

## The 5-5-5 Method (30 min/day)

| Time | Action | What |
|------|--------|------|
| **5 min** morning | Comment on 3 LinkedIn posts | Canadian ops leaders |
| **5 min** afternoon | Comment on 2 LinkedIn posts | Consulting firm partners |
| **5 min** evening | Send 1 connection request | With personalized note |
| **15 min** anytime | Write or schedule 1 LinkedIn post | About ops/strategy |

**Why this works for introverts:** It's written. It's async. It's controlled. No improvisation. No rooms full of strangers. Just you, your keyboard, and your brain.

## The Connection Request (copy-paste, customize)

### Template 1: Peer (Canadian ops leader)
```
Hi [Name],

I came across your post about [topic] and it resonated with me — 
especially the part about [specific detail from their post].

I'm an operations leader who recently relocated to Canada and I'm 
building my network here. I'd love to connect and learn about your 
experience at [Company].

No pressure for a call — just happy to connect here.

Best,
Aman
```

### Template 2: Consulting partner
```
Hi [Name],

I've been following [Firm]'s work in [practice area] and found your 
recent post about [topic] particularly insightful.

I'm an operations executive with 8 years of experience scaling 
multi-site operations and leading M&A integration. I'm exploring 
consulting opportunities in Vancouver and would value connecting.

Best,
Aman
```

### Template 3: KPU alumni
```
Hi [Name],

Fellow KPU grad here — I completed my Post-Bacc in Technical 
Management & Services last year. I saw you're working at [Company] 
and wanted to connect with fellow alumni in the Vancouver area.

Would love to stay in the KPU network.

Best,
Aman
```

### Template 4: Someone at a target company
```
Hi [Name],

I'm very interested in [Company]'s work in [area] and I noticed 
you're on the [team] there. I'd love to connect and learn more 
about the culture and work there.

I have 8 years of operations experience and I'm exploring 
opportunities in [area]. No ask — just genuinely interested in 
connecting.

Best,
Aman
```

## LinkedIn Content Strategy (for introverts)

**The rule:** You don't need to be a thought leader. You need to be visible.

### What to post (2-3x/week)

| Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Lesson learned** | "One thing I learned scaling operations from 3 to 70 people..." | Shows experience. Builds credibility. |
| **Industry observation** | "I've been thinking about how [trend] affects ops teams..." | Shows you're thinking. Invites conversation. |
| **Question** | "What's the biggest challenge you face in [area]?" | Invites engagement. Low effort for you. |
| **Article share** | "Interesting read on [topic]. My take: [1 sentence]" | Shows you're reading. Low effort. |
| **Celebration** | "Congrats to [person/company] on [achievement]" | Shows you're connected. Positive signal. |

### What NOT to post
- Political opinions
- Complaints about past employers
- "I'm looking for work" posts (desperate signal)
- Generic motivational quotes (noise)

### The 15-Minute Post Formula

1. **5 min:** Think of one thing you learned this week about ops/strategy
2. **5 min:** Write 3-5 sentences about it. Keep it simple.
3. **5 min:** Post it. Done.

**Example post:**
```
One thing I learned scaling operations from 3 to 70 people:

The biggest bottleneck isn't systems or processes — it's 
communication. When you're small, everyone knows everything. 
When you're 70, information silos form overnight.

The fix: weekly 15-minute standups per department + a shared 
dashboard that everyone can see. Simple. Effective. Scalable.

What's your biggest operations bottleneck?
```

## The Daily Routine (spoon-feed)

```

08:05 — Comment on 3 posts from Canadian ops leaders.

12:00 — Lunch break. Open LinkedIn.
12:05 — Comment on 2 posts from consulting partners.
12:10 — Done with afternoon networking.

17:00 — Evening. Open LinkedIn.
17:05 — Send 1 connection request with personalized note.
17:10 — Done with evening networking.

20:00 — Optional: Write 1 LinkedIn post (15 min).
```

**Total: 30 minutes/day.** That's it. Consistency beats intensity.

---

## The Philosophy (spoon-feed)

Think of networking like a pipeline:

```
LinkedIn Profile (your store) → Content (your product) → Comments (foot traffic) → DMs (checkout)
```

You don't need to walk into a room and shake hands. You need to:

1. **Make your LinkedIn profile work while you sleep** (your store is open 24/7)
2. **Write 2-3 posts per week** about ops/strategy (your product on display)
3. **Comment on 5 posts per day** from people you want to know (foot traffic)
4. **DM 2-3 people per week** with genuine questions (checkout)

The goal isn't to meet 50 people. It's to meet 3-5 people who remember your name.
