# COMPENSATION DEEP DIVE — Total Comp Framework

## Purpose
Pre-load total compensation intelligence for every target company BEFORE an offer arrives. Understand signing bonus, annual bonus mechanics, merit cycles, promotion bands, equity structures, and benefits so you know what to negotiate and when.

## Signing Bonus (By Tier)

| Tier | Typical Range | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| MBB | $25K-$50K | Standard for experienced hires. Can push $50K-$75K for top MBAs or niche operators |
| Big4 Strategy | $10K-$30K | More common at senior manager level. Less consistent at manager |
| Big4 Core | $5K-$15K | Often discretionary or relocation-based |
| Next-Tier | $5K-$25K | Negotiable if you have competing offers |
| Boutique | $0-$15K | Less common; more variable |

### Negotiation Levers
- **Have competing offer = 2-3x standard signing bonus**
- **Relocation:** If commuting/partial relocate, frame as "temporary transition costs" — some firms have separate relocation budgets
- **Clawback:** Standard is 1-2 years. If you leave before, you repay pro-rata. Push for 1-year or no clawback

## Annual Bonus Mechanics

### Formulas by Firm Type

| Firm Type | Bonus Type | Typical % | Formula | Payout Timing |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| MBB | Performance-based | 15-35% of base | Individual rating × firm performance multiplier | November/December (fiscal year end) |
| Big4 Strategy | Performance-based | 10-25% of base | Individual rating (1-5) × utilization × firm multiplier | June/July (fiscal varies) |
| Big4 Core | Performance + utilization | 5-15% of base | Heavy utilization weighting (target 85%+) | June/July |
| Next-Tier | Performance-based | 10-20% of base | Mix of individual + team + firm | Varies |
| Boutique | Discretionary | 0-20% of base | Owner/partner discretion | Varies |

### What To Ask
- "What was the average bonus payout as % of target for my level in the last 2 years?"
- "What's the utilization target for bonus eligibility?"
- "Is bonus capped or uncapped?"
- "What's the firm performance multiplier look like in a normal year?"

### Bonus Timing Strategy
- If hired mid-year, bonus is often pro-rated or forfeited for first year
- Ask for a **guaranteed first-year bonus** (signing bonus often serves this purpose)
- If no signing bonus, negotiate a first-year minimum bonus guarantee

## Merit Cycle & Promotion Timing

| Firm | Merit Review | Promotion Cycle | Typical Increase |
|------|-------------|----------------|------------------|
| McKinsey | Annual (June) | 2-3 years per level | 10-20% |
| BCG | Annual (varies by office) | 2-3 years | 10-15% |
| Deloitte | Annual (June) | 2-3 years (Sr Mgr → Mgr Director is hardest) | 8-15% |
| EY | Annual (July) | 2-3 years | 8-15% |
| PwC | Annual (July) | 2-4 years | 8-12% |
| Accenture | Annual (August) | 2-3 years | 8-15% |

### Key Insight
- **Promotion from Manager → Senior Manager is where comp jumps significantly** (often 20-30% increase)
- **Plateau at Senior Manager** is common (4-7 years before partner/MD)
- **External hire at Senior Manager is faster path to Partner** than promoting from within

## Equity / Long-Term Incentive (LTI)

| Firm | Equity for Experienced Hires? | Typical Structure |
|------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| MBB | Partner/MD only | Capital contributions, profit share |
| Big4 | Partner/MD only | Capital units, profit share |
| Accenture | Yes, at Senior Manager+ | RSUs, stock purchase plan |
| Boutique | Occasionally at Director+ | Phantom stock, profit share |
| Startups (S-pipe) | Yes, common | Options or RSUs at all levels |

### Key Questions
- "Is there an equity or long-term incentive program at this level?"
- "What's the vesting schedule?"
- "What was the last valuation / stock price?"
- "Is there a secondary market for shares?"

## Benefits Deep Dive (Canadian Context)

### Standard (Nearly Universal)
| Benefit | Typical Coverage |
|---------|-----------------|
| Extended health & dental | 80-100% coverage, family included |
| Vision | $400-$600 every 2 years |
| Life insurance | 1-2x salary |
| Disability (short-term) | 60-70% salary, 3-6 months |
| Disability (long-term) | 60-70% salary until 65 |
| RRSP matching | 3-6% of salary (varies heavily) |

### Differentiators (Not All Firms Offer)
| Benefit | Firms | Value |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Health spending account | Deloitte, EY, PwC, KPMG | $500-$2,000/yr |
| Wellness subsidy | McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte | $500-$1,500/yr |
| Paid sabbatical | MBB only | 4-8 weeks after 5-7 years |
| Home office budget | Post-COVID common | $500-$2,000 one-time |
| Professional development | All consulting | $2K-$15K/yr (varies) |
| Cell phone + laptop | All | Standard |
| Paid leave (vacation) | All | 3-5 weeks (increasing with tenure) |
| Parental leave | Big4/MBB | 12-26 weeks top-up (varies by province) |

### RRSP / Pension — Critical Differentiator
| Firm | Match | Vesting |
|------|-------|---------|
| Deloitte | 4% base | Immediate |
| EY | 3-5% base | Immediate |
| PwC | 4% base | Immediate |
| KPMG | 3-5% base | Immediate |
| Accenture | 4-6% base | 1-2 year vest |
| MBB | 5-7% base | Immediate |

### Hidden Perks
- **CPA/CFA/other certification fees** — Big4 often covers tuition + exam fees for professional designations
- **Travel points** — if you travel for client work, you keep the points
- **Referral bonus** — $2K-$10K per successful referral
- **Lunch/dinner** — Big4 often covers meals when working late (especially audit/advisory, less so strategy)

## Total Comp Calculation Template
```
TOTAL COMP = Base × (1 + Bonus%) + Signing Bonus(annualized) + Benefits Value + Equity/yr

Example: Deloitte Sr Mgr at $170K base, 20% bonus, $20K signing (annualized over 2yr = $10K), benefits ~$10K
= $170K + $34K + $10K + $10K
= ~$224K total comp Year 1
```

## Pre-Offer Intelligence Checklist
For each target company, before SHOOT, gather:
- [ ] Glassdoor salary range for target role level
- [ ] Blind / Reddit threads on bonus payout reliability
- [ ] LinkedIn salary data (if available)
- [ ] Recent funding/earnings (affects bonus pool)
- [ ] Promotion velocity (are people stuck at a certain level?)
- [ ] Benefits booklet (sometimes public on company site)
- [ ] RRSP match percentage
- [ ] Signing bonus range for experienced hires
- [ ] Utilization target for bonus eligibility
- [ ] Average tenure of people at target level
