# COVER LETTER EVALUATION CRITERIA

**Source:** Adapted from OPS_EVAL_CRITERIA.md — same evidence-based philosophy, applied to narrative/voice rather than resume structure.

---

## Core Principle

Evaluates a cover letter the way a hiring manager reads one: **does this feel like it was written specifically for us, by someone who already thinks like we do?** Every score must reference specific text from the cover letter. No vibes. All evidence.

The cover letter's job is NOT to repeat the resume. It's to answer: *"Why this company, why this role, why now — and why Aman specifically?"*

---

## Categories

### 1. Company Alignment (0-40 points)

**What this measures:** How deeply the letter mirrors the company's DNA — language registry, cultural cues, strategic initiatives, insider intelligence. The reader should think "this person has done their homework AND already speaks our language."

| Score Band | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **35-40** | Letter reads like an insider wrote it. Company-specific vocabulary from language registry woven naturally. References ongoing initiatives, recent news, leadership changes, product launches, or industry positioning. Not a single generic sentence that could apply to any company. The reader feels "how does this person know THAT about us?" |
| **25-34** | Strong alignment. Company keywords present. Industry-aware. One or two specific company references. Most sentences feel specific, not templated. |
| **15-24** | Some company-specific language but mixed with generic phrases. Could be for a competitor with minor edits. |
| **5-14** | Mostly generic business language. One or two company mentions but feel inserted rather than integral. |
| **0-4** | Clearly a template. No company-specific content. "Dear Hiring Manager," generic opening, no reference to the company's work, culture, or industry. |

**Evidence must reference:** Exact phrases from company language registry that appear in the letter, mentions of specific initiatives/products/values, cultural signal accuracy.

**Critical rules:**
- A letter that opens with "I am writing to apply for [role] at [company]" caps at 15 (generic opening)
- The letter must demonstrate Live Intelligence Alchemy — weaving company news or insider knowledge into the narrative
- If the same letter could be sent to a competitor without changing a paragraph, cap at 10
- Archetype must match the resume's chosen archetype (A/B/C) — drift deducts

---

### 2. Narrative Flow & Voice (0-30 points)

**What this measures:** Does the letter tell a coherent story? Does Aman's voice come through authentically? Is there a narrative arc — hook, connection, conviction, close — rather than a dry list of qualifications?

| Score Band | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **25-30** | Compelling narrative arc. Opens with a hook that connects Aman's story to the company's mission. Middle section builds the bridge between what he's done and what they need. Close is confident, specific, and forward-looking. Voice is authentic — reads like a human wrote it, not a ChatGPT template. The reader thinks "I want to meet this person." |
| **15-24** | Good structure with clear arc. Some narrative flair but dips into resume-listing. Voice is professional but could be warmer. Hook is decent but not magnetic. |
| **8-14** | Functional structure (opening, body, close) but feels formulaic. Mostly "I did X, Y, Z" without connecting it to company context. No hook. Voice is neutral/templated. |
| **1-7** | No narrative structure. Bullet-style listing of qualifications. Robotic tone. "I am a results-oriented professional with X years of experience in..." |
| **0** | Incoherent or missing. |

**Evidence must reference:** Specific phrases from each paragraph, how the narrative connects Aman's past → company's present → mutual future.

**Critical rules:**
- The "One True Pitch" ("I built something from nothing, scaled it to 70 people...") is a hook — but must be adapted to the specific company, not copy-pasted verbatim
- If the letter reads like a cover letter generated by mass-market AI tools, cap at 10
- Show-don't-tell: "I bring a builder's mindset" is weaker than "I built a healthcare business from 3 to 70 people, led the tech transformation myself, and delivered a $17M exit — then I stepped back and asked: where does this skillset create the most value next?"
- Archetype voice must be consistent: Builder (A) uses action-forward language, Strategist (C) uses analytical/framework language

---

### 3. Format, Tone & Anti-Pattern Compliance (0-20 points)

**What this measures:** Professional polish, adherence to standard cover letter conventions, absence of red-flag language.

| Score Band | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **18-20** | Perfect format. Correct header (`[NAME]` / Date / Vancouver, BC). Professional closing with full contact info. Appropriate length (3-5 paragraphs, 250-400 words). Tone matches company culture (formal for Big4, warm for startups). Zero anti-patterns. |
| **12-17** | Good format. Minor issues (slightly long/short, tone slightly off for the company). No anti-patterns. |
| **6-11** | Format issues present (missing header element, wrong length, inappropriate tone). One anti-pattern detected. |
| **1-5** | Multiple format issues. Several anti-patterns. Reads unprofessional or careless. |
| **0** | Unusable — no format, no closing, or contains red-flag language. |

**Evidence must reference:** Specific format elements, tone indicators, anti-pattern violations.

**Critical rules:**
- **Immutable:** No immigration/work permit/PR language under any circumstance. -5 if detected.
- **Immutable:** No unsupported claims (claims not traceable to Master Corpus). -3 per claim.
- **Immutable:** Archetype must match the resume. -3 if drifted.
- **Immutable:** If multi-role, cross-reference to other application(s) must be present. If missing, -3.
- Generic opening ("I am writing to apply") is an anti-pattern. -3.
- Overly humble language ("I believe I could be a good fit", "I hope to hear back") weakens conviction. -2.
- Salary expectations mentioned without being asked. -3.
- Negative language about past employers. -5 (immediate discard signal).

---

## Score Calculation

```
Total = Company Alignment (40) + Narrative Flow & Voice (30) + Format, Tone & Anti-Patterns (20)
       = 90 maximum

Pass threshold:   35 points (recruiter would read and think "worth interviewing")
Warning:         25-34 points (needs iteration before shipping)
Fail:            <25 points (rewrite from scratch)

Deductions:       Applied on top of score, floor at 0
```

---
