### The Hard Truth About Leveling

**Big4 Canada discounts industry experience by ~2 levels when hiring from outside consulting.** This means:

| Your Real Experience | Big4 Level They Offer | Title They Assign |
|---|---|---|
| 2-3 years industry | Analyst/Consultant | "Analyst" |
| 4-6 years industry | Senior Consultant | "Senior Consultant" |
| 7-10 years industry | **Manager** | **"Manager"** |
| 10+ years industry | Senior Manager | "Senior Manager" |

With 7+ years internal consulting / ops leadership, you:
- **Can get Senior Manager** at a boutique or firm hungry for your expertise
- **Will likely get Manager** at Big4 (standard experienced hire entry point)
- **Might get Senior Manager** at Big4 if the story lands perfectly and someone champions you

**This is not a judgment of your ability.** It's a market standard. Take Manager at Big4 → promote to Sr Manager in 18-24 months → life is good.

### The "Normal Consultant" Question

You said "not greedy for senior position, normal would do." Here's the truth:

- A "Consultant" at Big4 is ~2-4 years experience and $70K-$90K
- A "Manager" at Big4 is ~5-8 years experience and $110K-$160K
- You are Manager level, not Consultant level

**Apply for Manager roles, not Consultant roles.** If you apply for "Consultant" (which is entry-level), recruiters will think you're either underqualified or confused. Manager is the correct entry point for your experience.

## 5. THE RECRUITER PLAYBOOK — What to Give an Agency

This solves your question about "tailored resume vs generic for recruiter."

**Give recruiters a MASTER RESUME that shows 3 personas simultaneously:**

```
AMAN — Operations & Strategy Leader

PERSONA A: Builder-Entrepreneur
  "Built healthcare company 3→70 FTE, 32 locations, $17M exit"
  → For: Startups, PE-backed firms, founder-funded consulting

PERSONA B: M&A Integration Leader  
  "Directed full-cycle acquisition — due diligence through Day 100 integration"
  → For: Big4 (Deloitte, EY, KPMG), Accenture consulting

PERSONA C: Operations Transformer
  "Scaled operations from zero to multi-site, led tech transformation"
  → For: Internal strategy roles, healthtech, operations consulting
```

**How to structure it:**
1. **Header** — Name, title, contact. Generic enough for any persona.
2. **Summary** — 3-line "Swiss Army Knife" that mentions all 3 strengths
3. **Experience** — Laid out chronologically with bullets that hit all 3 personas
4. **Skills** — Broad list covering consulting, operations, tech, leadership

**Key difference from tailored resume:** A tailored resume has 1 persona and hides the others. A recruiter resume keeps all 3 visible so the recruiter can lead with whatever the client needs.

**Recruiter query:** "I'm an operations leader with 7+ years building and selling a healthcare company. I'm looking for consulting roles at Manager level in Vancouver — strategy, operations, or healthtech. Where do I fit?"
