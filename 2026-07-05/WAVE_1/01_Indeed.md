### 5 Q&A with Specific Answers

**Q1: Walk me through a complex integration you managed.**
> Skyfly Global acquired a multi-location clinic group in Arizona. They ran on Athenahealth, we ran on eClinicalWorks. Two completely different scheduling protocols, billing cycles, and team cultures. I built a 90-day integration plan: first 30 days focused on data migration and compliance alignment (Day 1 readiness), days 30-60 on workflow standardization and cross-training (synergy capture), days 60-90 on governance rhythms and performance tracking (Day 100 priorities). The key was establishing a weekly workstream cadence with clear owners and escalation paths. We hit every milestone on schedule.

**Q2: How do you align cross-functional teams without direct authority?**
> Three tools: (1) Structured governance — I build a cadence of workstream meetings with clear escalation paths, so issues surface before they become crises. (2) Shared metrics — every function tracks the same integration milestones, so we're aligned on what "done" looks like. (3) Relationship capital — I invest the first week in one-on-ones with every functional lead to understand their priorities, constraints, and pain points. When you understand what keeps them up at night, you can frame your requests in terms of their goals, not yours.

**Q3: Describe a time you identified an integration risk early.**
> During the diligence phase of a clinic acquisition, I noticed their patient scheduling system had no overlap with our RCM platform — a hard cutover would have caused a 3-week billing gap. I flagged it during the governance meeting, proposed a phased migration where both systems ran in parallel for 30 days, and built a reconciliation framework to catch discrepancies daily. The integration stayed on schedule, and revenue recognition was uninterrupted.

**Q4: How do you approach building an integration playbook?**
> I start with the end state: what does success look like at Day 100? Then I work backward: what needs to be true at Day 1, Day 30, Day 60, Day 90? Each milestone has clear owners, dependencies, and a "definition of done." The playbook is a living document — it gets updated at every governance review based on what's actually happening on the ground. The goal is not a perfect plan but a structured framework that makes course corrections visible and fast.

**Q5: Why Indeed, and why this role?**
> Indeed is at an inflection point — transforming from a job search engine into an AI-powered matching marketplace. That transformation requires exactly the kind of integration thinking I practice: aligning diverse workstreams, building operational frameworks from scratch, and ensuring strategic execution doesn't get lost in operational complexity. The Sr. Manager role sits at the center of that work — connecting corporate development, operations, and cross-functional execution. It's the intersection of everything I've built my career on.

### 3 STAR Stories

**STAR 1 — Integration Governance (Profitability)**
- **Situation:** Skyfly acquired 3 clinic groups in 18 months with no standardized integration process
- **Task:** Build a repeatable integration framework that could scale across acquisitions
- **Action:** Designed a 90-day governance model with weekly workstream cadences, milestone dashboards, and tiered escalation paths. Documented every process as a playbook entry.
- **Result:** 100% of subsequent acquisitions hit Day 1 readiness milestones. Reduced integration time from 6 months to 90 days.

**STAR 2 — Cross-Functional Alignment (Credibility)**
- **Situation:** Post-acquisition, clinical and operations teams had conflicting priorities — clinicians wanted system familiarity, ops wanted standardization
- **Task:** Align both teams around a unified integration timeline without direct authority over either
- **Action:** Built a shared milestone tracker with input from both sides. Negotiated a phased approach: clinical protocols first, then billing systems, then scheduling workflows.
- **Result:** Both teams felt heard. Integration completed on schedule with zero turnover in clinical staff.

**STAR 3 — Risk Identification (Visibility)**
- **Situation:** During a large clinic integration, discovered a compliance gap in patient data handling between two systems
- **Task:** Resolve before regulatory exposure occurred
- **Action:** Flagged in governance meeting within 24 hours. Built a cross-functional task force with clinical, IT, and legal. Designed a temporary process bridge while the permanent fix was built.
- **Result:** Zero compliance incidents. The bridge process was later adopted as the standard for all future integrations.
