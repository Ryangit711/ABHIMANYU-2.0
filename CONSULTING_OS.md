## Core Truth (Embedded, Never Repeat)

**Aman is not an operator who wants to be a consultant. Aman is a consultant who happened to operate.** Every system he built — from 3 to 70+ employees, from $0 to $4M ARR, from 1 location to 32 — was a consulting engagement that he also had to live inside. The difference between a consultant and an operator is: the consultant walks away after the deck. Aman stayed and made it real. That is rarer. That is more valuable.

The consulting industry's deepest unspoken need: **people who can both think it AND build it.** That is Aman's exact profile.

## Section 2: Consulting Firm Alchemy — Every Major Firm (Agnostic)

### Tier 1: MBB (McKinsey, BCG, Bain)

**McKinsey**
- DNA: Problem-solving aristocracy. The case is sacred. Intellectual firepower first, everything else second.
- What they read for: Structured thinking, hypothesis-driven approach, team leadership signals
- Aman → Them: Led $17M acquisition — that's a case study. Managed 5 BUs through OKR cycles — that's engagement management. Board-level reporting — that's partner communication.
- Them → Aman: Brand signal that opens every future door. Methodology training unmatched. Exit options: CEO track, PE, VC.
- Alchemy: Every bullet = "Structured [X], analyzed [Y], delivered [Z]." Lead with frameworks. Use consulting vocabulary (hypothesis, workstream, deliverable, stakeholder). Never say "I managed" — say "I led the engagement."
- Best entry: Implementation / Digital / Operations practices (less pedigree-gated than Strategy)

**BCG**
- DNA: Intellectual collaboration. Smarter together. Hypothesis-driven with creative flair.
- What they read for: Original thinking, collaborative examples, intellectual curiosity
- Aman → Them: Built novel solutions (8 systems → 1 platform) from scratch — shows creative problem-solving. 5 BU partnership shows cross-team collaboration.
- Them → Aman: Intellectual home. Thesis-driven culture. More autonomy than McKinsey.
- Alchemy: Frame everything as "My hypothesis was [X]. To test it, I [Y]. The data showed [Z], so we [W]." Show the thinking, not just the result.

**Bain**
- DNA: Results-oriented, hands-on, "results not reports." Closest to implementation consulting.
- What they read for: Demonstrable impact, client relationship depth, action orientation
- Aman → Them: Doesn't just advise — builds. That's Bain's exact sweet spot. 32 locations built, not recommended.
- Them → Aman: Most natural fit of the three. Bain values what you DID, not where you studied.
- Alchemy: Every bullet ends with a number. "Built [system] that [metric] resulting in [impact]." No theoretical frameworks — pure implementation evidence.

### Tier 2: Big4 (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC)

**Deloitte**
- DNA: Scale + Purpose. Largest 100% Canadian-owned professional services firm. "Make an impact that matters."
- Practices relevant to Aman: Strategy & Operations (generalist), Technology Strategy & Transformation (tech-enabled), Operate (delivery/implementation)
- Aman → Them: CRM architecture design, revenue forecasting cadence, M&A integration leadership, cross-functional program management, OKR implementation
- Them → Aman: Massive scale — 100s of concurrent roles. Can move between practices. Deep Canadian market presence. Strong AI/modernization focus (matches Aman's tech-ops blend).
- Alchemy: Lead with versatility. Deloitte values people who can flex across practices. Frame as: "Strategy thinker who can also deliver. Tech-savvy operator who also advises."

**EY**
- DNA: Quality + Ambition. "Building a better working world." Strongest in transformation delivery.
- Practices: EY-Parthenon (Strategy, most prestigious), Performance Improvement (Ops), Technology Consulting, Transactions (Deal advisory)
- Aman → Them: $17M acquisition = Transaction advisory gold. Systems consolidation = Performance Improvement. 3→70 scale = People Advisory.
- Them → Aman: Parthenon brand = MBB-equivalent on resume. Strong Canadian practice with Vancouver presence. Excellent for deal-adjacent strategy roles.
- Alchemy: Lead with the acquisition story — it's EY's native language (Transactions + Strategy combined). Then pivot to ops infrastructure building.

**KPMG**
- DNA: Trust + Clarity. Risk-aware, governance-focused. Strong in regulated sectors.
- Practices: Strategy, Risk Consulting, Deal Advisory, Technology Consulting
- Aman → Them: Board-level governance reporting. Risk-aware operations design. OKR as control framework.
- Them → Aman: Best for risk-adjacent operations roles. Strong public sector practice (matches security clearance timeline). Less prestige-gated than Deloitte/EY.
- Alchemy: Use governance language. "Designed control frameworks for cross-BU operations." "Built reporting cadence that enabled board-level oversight."

**PwC**
- DNA: Human-led, Tech-powered. People + technology together.
- Practices: Operations Advisory, Deals, Technology Consulting, Risk
- Aman → Them: 3→70 people scale = human-led operations. CRM architecture = tech-powered. Operations transformation = both.
- Them → Aman: Strong people analytics practice. Trust-based culture. Good for operations strategy roles.
- Alchemy: Lead with the people + systems combination. "Built operations infrastructure that scaled teams 23x while keeping culture intact."

→ Lead with frameworks. Use consulting vocabulary. Frame every bullet as hypothesis → analysis → recommendation.

### If JD mentions: M&A / INTEGRATION
→ Lead with $17M acquisition — due diligence, integration, talent retention, operational continuity.
**Perfect for:** Deal Advisory, Transaction Services, Corporate Development.

### If JD mentions: STARTUP / SCALE-UP
→ Lead with 3→70 story. Show you know what it takes to build from nothing.
**Perfect for:** Startup roles, growth-stage companies, VC-backed firms.
