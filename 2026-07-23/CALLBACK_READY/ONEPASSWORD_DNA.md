## 4. THREE STAR STORIES — SkyflyMD Mapped to SaaS Implementation

### STAR 1: Built the Onboarding Deployment System from Scratch

**Situation:** SkyflyMD was scaling from 5 to 32 locations through acquisitions. Each new location required onboarding 10-50 staff onto clinical systems (EHR, billing, scheduling). The process was entirely manual — spreadsheets, email threads, tribal knowledge. Average onboarding took 3+ weeks and introduced errors that cascaded into patient care.

**Task:** Design and implement a scalable deployment workflow system that could handle 5x volume without proportional headcount increases. I was responsible for the full lifecycle: requirements gathering, platform configuration, workflow design, rollout, and team training.

**Action:**
- Mapped the entire onboarding lifecycle across 5 existing locations to identify the 12 standard steps every deployment required
- Identified 7 steps that were location-specific vs. 5 that could be universally standardized
- Configured the workflow platform with automated triggers at each handoff point (Sales → Implementation → Go-Live)
- Built custom reporting dashboards showing real-time deployment status per location
- Created onboarding playbooks and training materials for implementation specialists
- Ran a 2-week pilot with 2 locations, gathered feedback, iterated on 3 workflow steps before full rollout

**Result:** Onboarding time reduced from 3+ weeks to 9 days. System handled 5x volume (27 acquisitions integrated) without adding implementation team headcount. Error rate in patient data setup dropped 60%. Dashboard became the single source of truth for leadership capacity planning. This system remained the operational backbone through the $17M exit.

**Map to SaaS implementation:** This IS the Rocketlane story. Building an implementation workflow platform, configuring it for scale, creating playbooks, measuring time-to-value, and running pilot → iterate → scale.

### STAR 2: Designed the Cross-Functional Handoff Framework

**Situation:** As locations scaled, the handoff between Sales (who closed the acquisition), Implementation (who onboarded staff), and Operations (who ran ongoing support) was creating gaps. Patients would be "installed" but not "activated" — the system was live but no one was driving adoption. Support tickets spiked 3 weeks post-onboarding because no one owned the activation window.

**Task:** Create an accountability framework that ensured every transition in the customer lifecycle had a named owner, clear data triggers, and SLA expectations. This required buy-in from 4 departments, each with different KPIs and incentive structures.

**Action:**
- Conducted interviews with 15 people across Sales, Implementation, Ops, and Finance to map every handoff point
- Identified 7 handoff gaps, 3 of which had no owner whatsoever
- Designed a lifecycle accountability matrix: each stage had an owner, a trigger condition, a time-bound action, and an escalation path
- Partnered with Finance to align commission structures with activation milestones (not just installation)
- Built automated alerts when handoff SLAs were at risk of breach
- Presented framework to executive leadership with projected impact on support costs and activation rates

**Result:** 40% reduction in post-onboarding support tickets within 3 months. Activation rate (defined as "fully operational within 30 days") increased from 62% to 89%. Handoff completion rate reached 97%. The framework was adopted as the standard for all subsequent acquisitions.

**Map to SaaS implementation:** Directly mirrors the Sales → Implementation → Customer Success handoff design this role owns. Shows ability to build accountability frameworks across CX functions.

### STAR 3: Led Operational Readiness for Rapid Acquisition Integration

**Situation:** SkyflyMD was acquiring 3 practices in a 60-day window — each with different systems, staff sizes (15-40 people), and levels of technical sophistication. The operations team needed to onboard all 3 simultaneously without degrading service quality at existing locations.

**Task:** Coordinate cross-functional readiness for 3 concurrent system launches, including rollout plans, change management, documentation, and training. I was the operational lead responsible for all 3 integrations running in parallel.

**Action:**

- Standardized the onboarding playbook into a repeatable template with location-specific configuration slots

- Coordinated 12 cross-functional workstreams (IT setup, HR onboarding, clinical training, billing integration, etc.) across all 3 locations
- Held daily 15-minute standups with implementation leads to surface blockers in real time
- Identified a critical data migration risk at Location #2 three weeks before go-live and re-sequenced the timeline to avoid a patient data gap

**Result:** All 3 locations onboarded within the 60-day window. Zero patient data gaps. Average time-to-fully-operational was 11 days (vs. the 21-day target). The "rapid deployment kit" became the standard template for all future acquisitions. No degradation in service quality at existing locations during the integration period.
