## 3. FIVE Q&A — INTERVIEW QUESTIONS + MODEL ANSWERS

### Q1: "Tell me about yourself and why EvenUp."

**Model Answer:**
"I'm an operations executive who built the entire back-office infrastructure for a US healthcare practice roll-up. Started with 3 people, scaled to 70 across 32 locations in 4 states. I designed the billing systems, the claim tracking dashboards, the onboarding playbooks, and the revenue recognition pipeline. What draws me to EvenUp is the operational inflection point you're at. You've raised $385M, serve 2,000+ law firms, and recently shifted to per-case pricing. That's a fundamental change in how revenue flows through the business. It requires rethinking CS workflows, health metrics, the entire post-sales engine. I've built exactly these systems under hypergrowth pressure. And the mission matters — leveling the playing field for injury victims against insurers is exactly the kind of work I want to be part of."

### Q2: "How would you approach building the CS operational infrastructure for a company transitioning to per-case pricing?"

**Model Answer:**
"First, I'd map the entire customer lifecycle against the per-case revenue model. Per-seat is predictable revenue; per-case is volume-dependent, which means your CS workflows need to be optimized for throughput and activation, not just relationship management.

Three things I'd start with immediately:
1. **Redefine health metrics.** NPS and relationship warmth aren't enough. In a per-case model, the key signals are: cases uploaded per client, time-to-first-case after onboarding, feature adoption depth, and renewal correlation with case volume — not satisfaction scores.
2. **Build the dashboard infrastructure.** CS leadership needs real-time visibility into: are clients uploading cases consistently? Is there a usage drop-off that signals churn risk? Which firms are under-utilizing vs. over-utilizing?
3. **Redesign onboarding for time-to-first-value.** In per-case pricing, the faster a firm gets value from their first 10 cases, the stickier they become. I'd measure onboarding in hours-to-first-case, not days-to-completion.

At my last company, I built the patient intake tracking system that scaled from 50 patients/week to 400+ — same principle: instrument the workflow, surface the signals, act before the client knows there's a problem."

### Q3: "Describe a time you built a process from scratch that scaled significantly."

**Model Answer:**
"When I joined SkyflyMD, the billing department was two people manually entering charges. As we scaled from 3 to 70 employees across 32 locations, I built the centralized revenue operations function from nothing. That included: designing the charge capture workflow, building the claims tracking dashboard in Salesforce, implementing automated denial management, and creating the KPI reporting cadence for monthly business reviews.

The system went from processing $0 to managing $4M in annual revenue with 98%+ clean claim rate. When we exited, the buyer specifically called out the operational infrastructure as a reason for the valuation — because it meant they could integrate without rebuilding.

For EvenUp, the parallel is direct: you're at the stage where CS operations need to scale from 'a few people managing clients' to 'a systematic engine that handles 2,000+ firms and growing.' I've built that engine from zero. I know where the bottlenecks hide."

### Q4: "How do you use data to identify churn risk before a customer decides to cancel?"

**Model Answer:**
"I build leading indicators, not lagging ones. Churn is a lagging indicator — by the time a client says they're leaving, you've already lost them.

In a per-case model, the churn signals are in usage patterns: Is their case upload volume declining week-over-week? Has their time-to-first-case after renewal increased? Are they using fewer features? Are support tickets trending up (frustration) or trending to zero (disengagement)?

I'd build a client health score that weights: case volume trend (40%), feature adoption (25%), support engagement (20%), renewal timing (15%). Then automated alerts for any client whose score drops below threshold — not 'let's check in' but 'here's the specific intervention based on their specific pattern.'

At SkyflyMD, I built a claims denial dashboard that caught denials within 48 hours instead of 30 days. Same principle. The earlier you see the signal, the earlier you can act."

### Q5: "EvenUp's AI has had accuracy concerns in the press. How would you approach CS operations in that context?"

**Model Answer:**
"I'd lean into it. The press narrative about AI accuracy is real. From a RevOps perspective, that means quality metrics are a first-class KPI, not an afterthought.

I'd build the operational infrastructure to track: demand accuracy rates by firm, revision cycles per case, client satisfaction correlated with AI output quality, and time-to-resolution for quality issues. Then a systematic feedback loop that feeds quality data back to the product and AI teams — because if your CS ops team isn't surfacing quality patterns, the product team is flying blind.

The companies that win aren't the ones with perfect AI — they're the ones with the best feedback loops between customer experience and product improvement. I've built those loops before. At SkyflyMD, the billing denial data I surfaced directly improved our payer contract negotiations — same idea, different domain."

## 4. THREE STAR STORIES

### STAR 1: Building RevOps from Zero → $4M ARR

**Situation:** At SkyflyMD, revenue operations didn't exist as a function. Billing was two people on spreadsheets. Claims were tracked manually. Denials discovered 30+ days after submission. The company was scaling from 3 to 70 employees across 32 locations.

**Task:** Build a centralized revenue operations function that could handle 10x growth in claim volume without proportional headcount.

**Action:**
- Designed and implemented a Salesforce-based charge capture workflow, automating 80% of manual entry
- Built real-time dashboards: claims volume, clean claim rate, denial rate by payer, days-in-AR
- Implemented automated denial management alerts (flagged within 48 hours vs. 30-day manual cycle)
- Created monthly business review cadence with KPI reporting for leadership
- Trained 12 location managers on dashboard usage for local decisions

**Result:** Clean claim rate: 85% → 98%+. Days-in-AR: 45 → 22. Revenue ops scaled to support $4M ARR without adding billing headcount. Operational infrastructure was a key driver of the $17M exit valuation.

**EvenUp mapping:** Building CS RevOps for per-case pricing. Same principles: instrument the workflow, surface signals, act before the client knows there's a problem.

---

### STAR 2: M&A Integration Across 32 Locations

**Situation:** SkyflyMD acquired 5+ medical practices across 4 states in 18 months. Each had its own billing system, payer contracts, workflows, and culture. Claims were being rejected, staff confused, revenue dipped 15% post-acquisition per practice.

**Task:** Design and execute the operational integration playbook — standardize workflows across all 32 locations while maintaining service quality.

**Action:**
- Created a 90-day integration playbook: system migration, staff training, workflow standardization, quality metrics
- Built cross-functional integration team (ops, billing, IT, clinical) with weekly check-ins and escalation protocols
- Designed training curriculum that onboarded 40+ new staff on standardized workflows in 2 weeks
- Implemented unified Salesforce dashboards at each acquired practice — centralized reporting from day one
- Negotiated payer contract harmonization — standardized billing codes across all locations

**Result:** Integration time per practice: 6 months → 90 days. Revenue recovery post-acquisition: 3 months → 6 weeks. All 32 locations on unified systems within 12 months. Playbook became template for all future acquisitions.

**EvenUp mapping:** EvenUp is onboarding thousands of law firms, each with different workflows, case management systems, and operational maturity. Building scalable integration playbooks — not one-off solutions — is exactly what they need.

**Result:** 3→70 people, $0→$4M ARR, $17M exit. Zero operational breakdown that cost a client. Systems were the foundation the buyer acquired.

**EvenUp mapping:** EvenUp is at the same inflection — going from startup chaos to institutional-grade operations. They need someone who's lived through 3→70, not someone who's only managed at scale.
