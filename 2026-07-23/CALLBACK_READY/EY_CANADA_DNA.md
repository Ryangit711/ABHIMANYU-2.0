### Q2: "How do you approach synergy identification and validation?"

**Model Answer:**

> "I approach it in three layers. First, I map every cost center in both organizations and identify overlaps — staffing, technology, facilities, vendor contracts. That's the easy part. Second, I validate by running stand-alone cost analyses: what does each entity cost to operate independently vs. combined? The delta is your real synergy, not the optimistic number in the pitch book. Third, I track realization monthly — synergy targets without tracking are just wishes.
>
> In my last role, I identified $2.3M in operational synergies across 3 acquisitions by consolidating vendor contracts, standardizing EHR systems, and centralizing billing operations. I validated each line item through stand-alone costing, then built a monthly tracking dashboard that reported to the board. We hit 94% of synergy targets within 18 months."

### Q3: "Walk us through how you'd manage a carve-out engagement for a client."

**Model Answer:**

> "A carve-out is controlled chaos — you're separating a living organism while keeping both halves healthy. I'd start with the Separation Management Office structure: define scope, governance, workstreams (IT, Finance, HR, Operations, Legal), and the critical path to Day 1 readiness.
>
> From my experience building and then separating operations: the first 90 days are about defining what's shared and what's分离. TSA negotiations are critical — you need to price the services the carve-out entity will still need from the parent, set clear termination dates, and avoid the trap of 'we'll figure it out later.' Operating model design comes next: what does the standalone entity need to function without the parent's infrastructure?
>
> I've done this in practice — when we separated operations across state lines, I led the TSA development, designed the standalone operating model, and managed the first 6 months of independence. The key: over-communicate, under-promise on timelines, and build the integration playbook before you need it."

### Q4: "How do you handle presenting recommendations to C-suite stakeholders?"

**Model Answer:**

Partners at EY-Parthenon will respect the same directness with their clients."
